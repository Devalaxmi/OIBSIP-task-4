Mother Teresa

Mother Teresa, founded the Missionaries of Charity.

    Here's a view of Mother Teresa life:

  • 1910 - was born in Skopje at the time, part of the Ottoman Empire.
  • 1928 - Anjeze left home in 1928 at age 18 to join the Sisters of Loreto at Loreto Abbey in Rathfarnham, Ireland, to learn English with the intent of becoming a missionary
  • 1929 - She arrived in India in 1929 and began her novitiate in Darjeeling, in the lower Himalayas, where she learned Bengali and taught at St. Teresa's School near her convent
  • 1931 - She took her first religious vows on 24 May 1931. She chose to be named after Therese de Lisieux, the patron saint of missionaries
  • 1950 - Mother Teresa received Vatican permission for the diocesan congregation, which would become the Missionaries of Charity
  • 1962 - Indian government first recognized Mother Teresa by awarding her the Padma Shri, the fourth highest civilian award in India.
  • 1979 - receives Nobel prize.
  • 1997 - dies at the age of 87.
